Overview & Problem

Innovating accessible pupillometry solutions for cognitive assessment

As part of a research team studying pupillometry for Alzheimer‚Äôs, I collaborated with two mentors to explore the use of the FarRed spectrum in pupillometry, enabling the technique to be effective for all eye colors. While this novel method provided a breakthrough, there remained a significant challenge: ensuring accessibility for patients from low socioeconomic backgrounds, with low technology literacy, and from non-white ethnicities. To address this, I developed an eyepiece building instruction set linked by a QR code to help patients self-diagnose cognitive disorders, reducing pupillometer building error rates by 50%.

Main Insights

At-home testing should use tools that patients already have

From the exploration done on recent breakthroughs, we noticed that pupillometers should be inexpensive and allow users to use minimal equipment. An instruction set that makes use of the phone to help patients create their own pupillometer whilst also guiding them on how to attach it to their RGB camera would be cost-effective way of implementing pupillometry at home.
